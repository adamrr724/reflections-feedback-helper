# FY26 Feedback Guide

*Source: GitHub HR*

---

## Why Feedback is Important

Feedback is an important part of the way we learn and grow at GitHub. Effective feedback is specific and helps us develop an understanding of where Hubbers are in terms of their performance, development, and career.

Feedback from our peers, managers, and reports helps us approach our work with a new perspective, supports our growth and career goals, and enables us to have a greater impact. At GitHub, we have a high quality bar, and it's important that we regularly and consistently reflect, iterate, and improve to meet and exceed our goals.

---

## Opportunities to Give and Receive Feedback

We offer multiple opportunities for feedback. The Feedback tool in Workday is available year round and is a great way to get feedback from your manager and peers. There's no need to wait until a formal Reflections conversation to discuss feedback with your manager. You can also use Slack and your 1:1s to gather feedback.

Hubbers should request and provide timely Feedback to provide meaningful change and opportunities for improvement. We encourage Hubbers to ask for feedback from a wide set of providers; not just from those who they work with every day.

### Consider asking feedback from:

**Key GitHub partners:** Hubbers who worked with the Hubber in different situations and environments, and who can provide fair, balanced, objective information about results and how they were achieved.

**Individuals outside the immediate team:** Virtual or cross-team members who worked with the Hubber on projects that involved collaboration and who have deep knowledge of the work that the Hubber delivered.

**Peers and other managers:** Those who interacted with the Hubber in different ways and can provide objective input on how they performed.

---

## Benefits of Providing and Receiving Feedback

Feedback allows Hubbers to:

- **Expand professional impact** beyond their point of view
- **Receive positive and constructive feedback** to help reinforce positive behavior, course correct, and enable teams to work more effectively toward their goals
- **Experience continuous learning** and supports Hubber professional performance and development goals

---

## Once You've Received Feedback, Consider:

- What steps you can take to learn from the Feedback and apply it to how you work so that you can achieve greater impact
- How to integrate the Feedback you received into upcoming Reflections
- What Feedback you received and how you incorporated the Feedback into your work and development plans
