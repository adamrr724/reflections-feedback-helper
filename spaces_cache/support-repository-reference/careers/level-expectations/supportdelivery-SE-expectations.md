# Support Delivery Support Engineer Metric Expectations

The purpose of this document is to establish transparently shared expectations between teams in Support Delivery regarding metrics and performance expectations. The intent of the `Weight (%)` column is to aid balancing the various criteria when managers assess the impact an individual has made throughout the year.

## Support Engineer II (Level 7)

Metric | Weight (%) | Rating scale | Evaluation Rubric (criteria)
---- | ---- | ---- | ---- | 
 Solved tickets | 30% | <ul><li>Exceptional</li><li>Successful</li><li>Slightly Lower</li><li>Lower Impact</li></ul> | Assessed by comparing solved tickets to the squad baseline. |
Ticket Resolution Time | 20% | <ul><li>Exceptional</li><li>Successful</li><li>Slightly Lower</li><li>Lower Impact</li></ul> | Measures the ability to resolve tickets efficiently; assessed by comparing individual resolution times to the squad baseline. Takes into account the volume of tickets an individual Supportocat is assigned to in comparison to the squad baseline.
Ticket complexity | 10% | <ul><li>Exceptional</li><li>Successful</li><li>Slightly Lower</li><li>Lower Impact</li></ul> | Assessed by comparing complexity level to the squad baseline. |
Documentation contribution | 10% | <ul><li>Exceptional</li><li>Successful</li><li>Slightly Lower</li><li>Lower Impact</li></ul> | Includes contributions to the Support KB and/or improvements to the public docs.  
Collaboration and Team Support / Tool and Process Improvements | 10% | <ul><li>Exceptional</li><li>Successful</li><li>Slightly Lower</li><li>Lower Impact</li></ul>  | Includes canned responses, collaboration in Slack helping answer team members' questions, participation in answering questions in the #support channel, participation in the Tool Swarm, participating in projects, interviews, buddy, etc etc. 
Training and Skill Development | 20% | <ul><li>Exceptional</li><li>Successful</li><li>Slightly Lower</li><li>Lower Impact</li></ul> | Considers required training and enablement sessions, GitHub certifications, squad badging, and contributions to enablement projects, etc.  

#### Reporting

- The [SE Comparison Report in PowerBI](https://github.com/github/support/blob/main/docs/level-expectations/Comparison-Metric-report.md) allows individuals to freely track their progress compared to an average for others assigned to their squad worldwide, and best align their impact with manager guidance as needed.

### Support Engineer Expectations

#### Support Delivery and Ticket Work

- Help the team maintain a minimum of 4.5 CSAT
- Help the team maintain a minimum 95% IR Met
- Provide screen share/calls as needed when appropriate to progress ticket troubleshooting with technical problems

#### Team Collaboration

- Prioritize squad-specific responses to crisis situations identified by Support Incident Coordination.
- Share ideas via Issues, Pull Requests, and/or participating in/submitting [Support Change Proposal](https://github.com/github/support/issues/new?assignees=&labels=change-proposal&projects=&template=change_idea.yml&title=Change%3A+)(s) to help identify pain points and champion growth within the program.
- Help keep our support documentation up to date via associated PRs and modifications as needed.

#### L&D

- Complete required training, including [Microsoft](https://thehub.github.com/learning/req-training/) as assigned for role.
- If needed to improve your ticket velocity, work towards gaining usage and troubleshooting knowledge across appropriate product suites/technologies customers are using/expecting help with (for example, GHAS, Actions, etc.) as best aligns with your Squad, role requirements/OARs, and/or career ambitions.

- Help team with ticket triage across their Squad(s) and pair with engineers on tickets where expertise is needed.

- Directly help improve Squad velocity (playbook/KB content, meetings, ticket smashes, etc...) for assigned Squad(s).

### Staff Support Engineers (Level 10) Expectations

Staff Support Engineers (Level 10) are accountable for technical leadership, squad coordination, and proactive enablement ensuring squad health, customer advocacy, and knowledge sharing are core to their impact.
Ticket Work

---
#### Ticket Work

- Routinely carry consistent and complex ticket workload, analyzing issues that require nuanced judgment and cross-team perspective.
- Act as a primary point of contact within the squad for technical escalations, ensuring timely resolution and visible follow-through.
- Engage in triage, trend analysis, and process improvement for squad ticketing patterns.

#### Documentation & Communication

- Encourage squad members to actively contribute to documentation. Coach processes for creating and updating both internal and external docs when needed.
- Teach and standardize processes for creating and updating both internal and external docs.
- Maintain up-to-date squad documentation, ensuring important and timely information is available and digestible in squad repos and communication channels.
- Communicate key squad updates and knowledge across teams and stakeholders.

#### Process Improvement & Collaboration

- Identify, evaluate, and drive process improvements across squads, sharing learnings and best practices to benefit the broader team.
- Encourage open communication and collaboration to remove barriers and streamline workflows within and between squads.
- Lead by example in cross-team initiatives, documenting and sharing outcomes.

#### Project Work & Initiatives

- Lead squad specific work to reduce TTR and IPX.
- Act as an expert mentor and lead contributor on squad and cross-team projects, representing squad needs and technical direction.
- Drive squad involvement in release readiness, supportability, and engineering triage meetings, identifying appropriate contributors for each project or initiative.
- Facilitate cross-functional partnerships and process improvements relevant to squad objectives.

#### Squad Health & Meetings

- Monitor squad health and momentum, initiating check-ins and facilitating engagement to resolve challenges and support squad goals.
- Proactively participate in and represent the squad in key internal (ticket smashes, release readiness, supportability and engineering triage) and external meetings/escalations, ensuring technical expertise and context are articulated.
- Surface squad trends, gaps, and improvement opportunities in appropriate forums.

#### Trainings & Skill Development

- Balance impactful contribution with opportunities for personal learning and knowledge sharing.

#### Outcomes

- Timely, visible resolution of squad requests and escalations.
- Documented updates and knowledge sharing in squad repos, Slack channels, and meetings.
- Active mentorship, training delivery, and enablement for squad members.
- Inclusion and representation in cross-team and squad-specific meetings.
- Continuous process improvement and documentation of best practices.
- Feedback loops and transparent reporting of squad health, trends, and deliverables.
