# Promotion Process in Support: Requirements & Review Steps
Promotion process in Support aligns with the [GitHub promotion process](https://thehub.github.com/hr/total-rewards/promotion-at-github/).

A successful promotion in the Support organization follows three key requirements.

In order of priority:

1. **Business Justification**
    * There must be a solid business case for adding a higher-level role to the team.
    * This is aligned as part of our **Support Operating Model Strategy** and typically determined at the beginning of the fiscal year.
    * Business justification is reviewed and communicated with **CSLT** to ensure alignment on business needs.
2. **Personal Readiness**
    * The individual must be prepared to operate at the next level.
    * This assessment follows a structured approval process: \
       **Manager Nomination → Support LT Review → CSLT Review → RLT Review → GitHub SLT Review**
    * Approval is required at every stage before moving forward.
    * If readiness concerns are identified at any stage, feedback is given to the manager for development planning.
3. **Finance Review**
    * Promotions must fit within the **half-yearly budget** for promotions.
    * The Finance team reviews the final prioritized list of approved promotions and determines affordability.
    * If a promotion nomination is deferred due to budget constraints, it will be **fast-tracked in the next cycle**, provided the individual continues to demonstrate readiness.

## **Promotion Expectations & Communication**

To **avoid misplaced expectations**, managers will **not inform employees** that they are being considered for promotion. Since approval is required at multiple levels, a promotion is **never guaranteed**.

## What Happens If a Promotion Is Not Approved

* **Business Justification Not Approved**
    * The manager will continue working with leadership to clarify the justification.
    * Normally, business needs are set in advance, but adjustments may occur based on evolving priorities.
* **Personal Readiness Not Approved**
    * If an individual does not pass a readiness review, feedback is provided directly to the manager.
    * The manager will incorporate this feedback into the **Individual Development Plan (IDP)** to address growth areas for a future promotion.
* **Finance Review Not Approved**
    * If a promotion is deprioritized due to budget constraints, the manager will be advised to **resubmit in the next cycle** at **top priority**.
    * If the individual remains at the required level of readiness, they will **bypass the business justification and readiness steps** in the next round.

## Possible Scenarios

1. **The individual is personally ready, but there is no business justification.**
    * The promotion **will not be approved**.
    * Leadership will continue assessing team needs in future planning cycles.
2. **There is business justification, but no one is ready for promotion.**
    * Instead of promoting prematurely, the focus will be on **accelerating growth opportunities** for the team to develop future leaders.
