# Set yourself up for success

Whether you're preparing for a new role, considering a career change, facing an unplanned transition, or want to make the most of your current role, a little preparation can set you up for future success.

## Reflections

Use tooling like [the reflections and feedback helper](https://github.com/adamrr724/reflections-feedback-helper) and [reflect-gpt](https://github.com/github/reflect-gpt) to assist with creating your reflection. Use these tools to create a general journal of your work on a weekly/bi-weekly basis and copy to a personal repository.

As part of the bi-annual reflection cycle, consider taking additional time to note your key achievements, skills gained and impactful projects to update your resume/LinkedIn to reflect these.

Colleagues who are already writing feedback for your reflection may also consider adding a public recommendation to your LinkedIn profile, or ask for a written reference on GitHub letterhead.

Example request:

> Hi Hubber, I'm working on my reflection and would love your feedback on our work together. Also, I'd like to ensure you remain part of my network—would you mind sharing your personal contact details? I'd be happy to reciprocate and share mine as well.

## Individual development plan

Identify your strengths and develop an [individual development plan](Supportocat%20IDP%20(Individual%20Development%20Plan).md) to unlock your full potential. Use the [Supportocat IDP helper Copilot space](https://github.com/copilot/spaces/github/981) to assist you in developing your plan. 

Consider how your next steps align with your goals and leverage your strongest skills.

Engage in [learning and development](../operational-processes/policies.md) to build your confidence and skills in the areas that you identify.

## Resources

Regularly save important documents:
- Payslips.
- Employment documentation.
- Save past reflections and manager feedback.
- Benefits info.
- Certifications or documents related to learning, like Microsoft Learn courses. Ensure you add your personal email address to your [Microsoft Learn](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/) account to retain certificates.
- Other HR documents you may need.
- Save `hr@github.com` for potential future contact.
- Worker documents stored in Workday.
- Personal contact information for colleagues and mentors.
- Alumni network connections.
- Ensure you can still access your accounts if you lose your laptop.
- Ensure you add a personal account or email to your [1Password](https://thehub.github.com/it/how-to/access-1-password/) business account.

Official documentation for [Leaving GitHub](https://thehub.github.com/hr/policies/leaving/) is available on The Hub and [GitBenefits](https://experience100.ehr.com/github/Global/Info-Resources/Leaving-GitHub).

## Be Organized

Regularly maintain your records and update your profile. Store important files and notes in a personal repository (digital/cloud storage) and use tools (like Copilot/Actions) to automate tasks, including adding your reflection to a personal repository.
