
## Support Delivery and Premium Support Hiring Directory Usage
This structure, https://github.com/github/support/careers/hiring has been resurrected to store all artifacts, from the Support organization, related to hiring new Supportocats.  The items stored in this location includes, but is not limited to, artifacts related to recruiting, interviewing and hiring.

The directory structure employed at this location is for use by the Support organization and is arranged with directories according to the teams within Support.  Each Support team will use their directory structure to store and maintain their job descriptions, interview related content and other artifacts related to the GitHub hiring process.

### Change Control of Job Descriptions located within this structure

Support Managers are responsible for creating, updating and maintaining the job descriptions for their teams. GitHub will be used to maintain updates to the job descriptions and to store the latest and greatest versions of the job descriptions for use in conjunction with HR and with internal and external recruiting purposes.

### Responsibilities

Managers construct and finalize job descriptions and coordinate with HR Talent Partners for updates to be entered into Greenhouse, etc.

Managers will use GitHub for version control of job descriptions

Pull Requests (PRs) will be used, with at least one separate approver, before changes are merged.

### Codility Technical Assessment

**Format:** Codility (asynchronous)  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Benchmark Score:** 60%

Candidates complete a 10-question multiple choice assessment covering CI/CD troubleshooting, code security, GitHub Actions, Copilot usage and privacy, and GHES diagnostics. Automatically scored by Codility.

**Materials:**
- [Codility Assessment Guide](technical-assessment/codility-assessment.md) - Full questions and correct answers

### Technical Debrief

**GitHub Values Assessed:** Customer-Obsessed, Ship to Learn, Growth Mindset, Own the Outcome, Better Together

A 45-minute live interview where candidates discuss their reasoning behind their answers to the multiple choice technical assessment. The interviewer probes understanding, diagnostic methodology, safety awareness, and communication skills. (~15 minutes after the interview for iCIMS feedback entry.)

**Debrief Goals:**
- **Authenticity:** Does the candidate truly understand their answers?
- **Technical Safety:** Does the candidate avoid risky or destructive actions?
- **Practicality:** Are the candidate's steps logical, efficient, and realistic?

**Materials:**
- [Technical Debrief Run Sheet](technical-debrief/runsheet.md) - Probing questions and good answer examples

**Scoring:** 36 points total (12 per question × 3 questions). Score bands:
- 31-36 = Exceptional → Hire
- 25-30 = Meets Expectations → Hire
- 0-24 = Does Not Meet Expectations → Do Not Hire
