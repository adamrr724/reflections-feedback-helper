Reflections are a chance to focus on what you achieved, how you achieved it, and what you learned. They help you align on goals, share insights, and grow from experience—turning reflection into forward momentum. The insights you uncover can spark new ideas, deepen your impact, and shape your approach to future challenges.

Use GitHub's Values, Leadership Principles, and Manager Fundamentals to guide the writing of your reflections.

## Question 1

**1. What results did you deliver, and how did you do it?**

- **Quantify your impact:** Describe outcomes you achieved using clear, measurable results.
- **Leverage of AI:** Share how you applied AI to enhance productivity or quality - for yourself, your team, or GitHub customers.
- **Security contributions:** Highlight how you prioritized and strengthened security for GitHub and its customers.
- **Culture in action:** Reflect on the behaviors that exemplify GitHub's culture and values.
- **People Management (people managers):** Explain how you coached for performance, supported growth, modeled culture, and contributed to team and org success.

## Question 2

**2. Reflect on recent challenges - what did you learn and how did you apply a growth mindset?**

- Describe the situation, and why it tested your skills or mindset.
- Describe the actions you took, and how you stayed open to learning or feedback.
- Describe how the experience shaped your thinking or approach to future challenges.

## Question 3

**3. What are your goals for the upcoming period?**

- Define 3-5 SMART (specific, measurable, achievable, relevant/realistic, and time-bound) goals with a clear focus on business outcomes.
- Goals should support the development of secure, scalable products and services.
- If you're a people manager, include your people management goal(s).
