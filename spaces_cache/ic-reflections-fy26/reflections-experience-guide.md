# FY26 The Reflections Experience Guide

Reflections are a structured opportunity for Hubbers to share what they achieved, how they achieved it, and what they learned. They help managers and Hubbers align on goals, celebrate progress, and grow from experience—turning insights into forward momentum. The insights uncovered can spark new ideas, deepen impact, and shape the approach to future challenges.

This process supports GitHub's culture of strategic progress through sound judgment and continuous feedback.

The Workday Reflections tool powers the Reflections Conversation framework, guiding discussions with three key questions that help Hubbers articulate their impact, behaviors, and future goals—ensuring each conversation stays focused, actionable, and aligned with our values.

## Reflections: What You Need to Know

**Reflections are your chance to:**

- Share what you accomplished, how you did it, and what you learned.
- Align with your manager on goals, celebrate progress, and identify growth opportunities.
- Turn your insights into action for future success.

**How it works:**

- You'll answer three questions in Workday about your impact, behaviors, and goals.
- Your manager reviews your reflection, adds feedback, and connects your work to GitHub's values and priorities.
- Together, you'll discuss your achievements, learning, and next steps in a dedicated Reflections conversation.

**Why it matters:**

- Reflections help you and your manager stay aligned on expectations and goals. They support continuous feedback, growth, and career development.
- The process is designed to spark new ideas, deepen your impact, and help you prepare for future challenges.

**What happens next:**

- After your conversation, your manager finalizes comments in Workday.
- You may be invited to clarify or expand your responses if needed.
- Use the momentum to plan a career fulfillment conversation focused on your aspirations and growth.

## Write a Reflection - Quick Guide

Reflections help you and your manager align on goals, celebrate progress, and identify growth opportunities. They focus on what you achieved, how you achieved it, and what you learned.

### 1. What did you achieve?

- **Describe your results:** Use clear, measurable outcomes.
  - Example: "Delivered X project two weeks early, resulting in a 10% increase in customer satisfaction."
- **Highlight impact:** Focus on the value created for customers and/or GitHub.
  - Example: "Automated a reporting process, saving the team 5 hours per week."

### 2. How did you achieve it?

- **Showcase behaviors and skills:** Reflect on how you worked, collaborated, and modeled GitHub's values.
  - Example: "Led weekly stand-ups to keep the team aligned and fostered open communication."
- **Consider feedback:** Include relevant input from peers or managers.
  - Example: "Acted on feedback to improve documentation clarity, reducing onboarding time for new hires."
- Keep in mind that what you achieved and how you achieved it are equally important.

### 3. What did you learn?

- **Share lessons and growth:** Describe challenges and how you overcame them.
  - Example: "Faced a technical setback, learned a new tool, and applied it to resolve the issue."
- **Apply a growth mindset:** Explain how the experience shapes your future approach.
  - Example: "After missing a deadline, I improved my time management by setting weekly milestones."

### 4. Set SMART Goals

- Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant/Realistic, Time-bound.
- Example: "Complete advanced security training by Q2 and implement two new protocols to reduce vulnerabilities."

### Tips

- **Be concise:** Focus on 2–3 key impact areas.
- **Use examples and metrics:** Quantify results where possible.
- **Connect to company values:** Show alignment with team and organizational goals.
- **Include feedback:** Demonstrate how you've acted on input.
- **Highlight collaboration:** Note contributions to team success.

### Special Circumstances

- **New hires:** Focus on onboarding and early achievements.
- **Role transitions or return from leave:** Reflect on past contributions and set future goals.

## Write a Reflection - Detailed Guide

### Reflection Questions

The process focuses on three questions:

**1. What results did you deliver, and how did you do it?**

- **Quantify your impact:** Describe outcomes you achieved using clear, measurable results.
- **Leverage of AI:** Share how you applied AI to enhance productivity or quality—for yourself, your team, or GitHub customers.
- **Security contributions:** Highlight how you prioritized and strengthened security for GitHub and its customers.
- **Culture in action:** Reflect on the behaviors that exemplify GitHub's culture and values.
- **People Management (people managers):** Explain how you coached for performance, supported growth, modeled culture, and contributed to team and org success.

**2. What did you learn and how did you apply a growth mindset?**

- Describe the situation, and why it tested your skills or mindset.
- Describe the actions you took, and how you stayed open to learning or feedback.
- Describe how the experience shaped your thinking or approach to future challenges.

**3. What are your goals for the upcoming period?**

- Define 3-5 SMART (specific, measurable, achievable, relevant/realistic, and time-bound) goals with a clear focus on business outcomes.
- Goals should support the development of secure, scalable products and services.
- If you're a people manager, include your people management goal(s).

### Importance of Feedback

Including feedback in your Reflections demonstrates that you actively seek input, learn from others, and are committed to continuous improvement. It shows how you've acted on feedback to grow your skills and increase your impact, making your reflection more meaningful and credible. Incorporating feedback also highlights your openness to collaboration and your alignment with team and company goals.

- Ask for feedback regularly—not just during Reflections—and consider input from peers, partners, and those outside your team.
- Review feedback for common themes and think about how you've acted on it or what you could do next.
- Include feedback in your Reflections that's most relevant to your growth and impact.
- Gather feedback informally (chats, emails, Slack) or formally (Workday Feedback tool); if you don't have much, reach out for more.
- Regularly seeking and using feedback shows a growth mindset and supports your ongoing development at GitHub.

### "What" and "How"

Performance is measured by impact and we continue to define impact as—what you achieved and how you achieved it. Reflections are your opportunity to describe your results and the behaviors or actions behind them and to make sure you are aligned with your manager.

**What — The results you deliver for your goals.**

Reflections are one of the most effective ways to prioritize and clarify performance expectations and drive meaningful results.

- A strong "what" involves setting ambitious, and SMART goals with a clear focus on business outcomes.
- Goals should align with team and business priorities and focus on delivering maximum value to customers and GitHub and support the development of secure, scalable products and services.
- If you're a people manager, this includes building a high performing, healthy team and the results delivered through manager expectations: modeling, coaching, and caring.

**How — Your behaviors and actions in pursuit of your goals. They influence how we work, interact and lead.**

- **How we work** — Stay close to our customers, model our values, and seek out and consider diverse perspectives in decisions and solutions.
- **How we interact** — Treat others with respect, act with integrity, and take accountability for your commitments and impact.
- **How we lead** — Create clarity and generate energy so others can succeed, and deliver success (leadership principles). For managers, this also means modeling our culture, coaching for performance and caring for individuals (manager fundamentals).

### "What" did you achieve?

In your Reflections, clearly highlight your progress and results for each goal, using specific examples and measurable outcomes. Consider:

- **Most impactful outcomes:** Quantify results with metrics or milestones (e.g., revenue, customer satisfaction, delivery speed, quality).
  - Example: "Reduced onboarding time by 30% through process automation."
- **Use of AI:** Describe how you leveraged AI to improve productivity or quality for yourself, your team, or customers.
  - Example: "Implemented AI-driven code reviews, increasing deployment speed."
- **Security contributions:** Explain how you prioritized and strengthened security for GitHub and its customers.
  - Example: "Introduced new authentication protocols, reducing security incidents."
- **Value creation:** Show how your work benefited customers and the company.
  - Example: "Launched a feature that increased customer retention by 15%."
- **Product and service impact:** Highlight contributions to secure, scalable products and services.
  - Example: "Optimized infrastructure to support 2x user growth."
- **Expertise:** Demonstrate end-to-end knowledge of your discipline.
  - Example: "Led cross-functional training sessions to upskill the team."

**For people managers**, clearly highlight your team's progress and results, using specific examples and measurable outcomes. Consider:

- **Team impact:** Quantify your team's key results with metrics or milestones.
  - Example: "My team launched a new feature that increased customer engagement by 20%."
- **Empowerment and accountability:** Describe how you modeled expectations and empowered your team to deliver results.
  - Example: "Modeled accountability by delegating sprint ownership, which improved delivery speed and team morale."
- **People development:** Share how you coached and supported growth and performance.
  - Example: "Coached a team member to take on a lead role, resulting in a successful project handoff."
- **Culture and care:** Highlight how you fostered a positive, inclusive, and high-performing environment by caring for your team.
  - Example: "Facilitated cross-team workshops to drive collaboration and supported team well-being through regular check-ins."

### "How" did you achieve it?

Showcase the behaviors and skills that drove your impact, using specific examples. Consider:

- **Living our values:** Demonstrate behaviors that reflect GitHub's culture.
  - Example: "Promoted transparency by sharing project updates in team channels."
- **Seeking diverse perspectives:** Involve others in decisions and solutions.
  - Example: "Consulted with the Accessibility team to improve our product design for all developers."
- **Staying close to customers:** Focus on understanding and meeting customer needs.
  - Example: "Regularly gathered user feedback to guide feature improvements."
- **Interacting with integrity:** Treat others with respect and take accountability.
  - Example: "Owned mistakes and worked collaboratively to resolve issues."
- **Embedding DI&B into behaviors:** Create inclusive teams at GitHub.
  - Example: "Regularly seek input from team members to ensure all voices are heard, especially those who are non-U.S. or may have differing perspectives."
- **Creating clarity and energy:** Help others succeed by communicating clearly and motivating the team.
  - Example: "Facilitated brainstorming sessions to energize the team around new ideas."
- **Building on others' work:** Collaborate and contribute to shared success.
  - Example: "Built on a teammate's solution to deliver a more robust final product."

**For people managers**, describe the behaviors and actions you used to drive your team's success, using specific examples. Consider:

- **Model:** Demonstrate company values and set clear expectations through your own actions.
  - Example: "Modeled transparency by openly sharing project goals and progress in team meetings."
- **Coach:** Support team growth by providing feedback, guidance, and development opportunities.
  - Example: "Coached team members through regular 1:1s, helping them set and achieve stretch goals."
- **Care:** Foster an inclusive, supportive environment and prioritize team well-being.
  - Example: "Managed time zone differences for global teams to ensure team members from different regions can participate effectively in team meetings and discussions."
- **Collaboration and trust:** Build strong relationships and encourage cross-team cooperation.
  - Example: "Facilitated collaboration with other departments to deliver a complex project on time."

### What did you learn?

Reflect on challenges and growth opportunities, applying GitHub's values and a growth mindset. Consider:

- **Lessons from experience:** Identify what you learned and how you might approach things differently for greater impact.
  - Example: "After a project delay, I learned to communicate risks earlier and involve stakeholders sooner."
- **Growth mindset:** Show how you turned setbacks into learning and future improvement.
  - Example: "When feedback highlighted gaps in my technical skills, I took an online course and applied new techniques to my next assignment."
- **Continuous improvement:** Explain how your learning will shape your approach in the coming months.
  - Example: "I plan to seek regular feedback and collaborate more closely with cross-functional teams to deliver better results."

### Set SMART Goals

Create goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant/Realistic and Time-bound.

| Component | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| Specific | The goal should be clear and well-defined. |
| Measurable | The goal should track progress and measure the outcome. |
| Achievable | The goal should be attainable given resources and constraints. |
| Relevant / Realistic | The goal should align with objectives and values and be practical and attainable given resources, skills and time. |
| Time-bound | The goal should have an end date to create momentum and focus. |

When writing your SMART goals:

- **Define success and measurement:** Clearly state what you want to accomplish and how you'll track progress.
  - Example: "Increase customer satisfaction scores by 10% within six months."
- **Align with priorities:** Make sure your goals support team or organizational objectives and are realistic given your resources and skills.
  - Example: "Complete training on new security protocols to support our product launch."
- **Collaborate and revisit:** Work with others to shape your goals and review them regularly to adjust as needed.
- **Use goal-setting techniques:** Try methods like "Working Backward" (start with the desired result and plan steps to achieve it) or the "Five Whys" (ask why the goal matters up to five times) to ensure your goals are relevant and actionable.

## Prepare for your Reflections Conversation

**Purpose:** The Reflections Conversation is a dedicated time to discuss your reflection, get feedback, and align on next steps. Regular check-ins throughout the year help maintain momentum and focus on progress.

**How to Prepare:**

- Review your Reflection including your manager's comments and any feedback you've received.
- Identify themes: Achievements, challenges, and growth areas.
- Prepare questions about impact, career fulfillment, team dynamics, or specific feedback.
- Be open — celebrate progress, learn from experience, and plan for what's next.

**During the Conversation:**

- Share what you achieved, how you did it, and what you learned.
- Discuss how your work connects to GitHub's culture, values, and priorities.
- Highlight collaboration and contributions to team success.
- Point out how you included DI&B and attention to security in your work.
- Explore opportunities for growth, learning, and greater impact.
- Align on goals for the next period.

**After the Conversation:**

- Continue the dialogue in regular 1:1s to stay aligned on goals and impact.
- Seek feedback from others—via Slack, email, live conversations, or the Workday Feedback tool—and use it to learn and grow.

**Tips for Conversations with your Manager:**

- Practice a growth mindset and share your thoughts openly.
- Speak to your collaboration and efforts to embed inclusion into your everyday work.
- Ask specific questions to gain clarity, learn ways to deliver greater results, or readiness for new responsibilities.
- Share what motivates you so your manager can help you maximize your strengths and look for opportunities to showcase your strengths beyond your team.
- Take time, if needed, to process feedback and request a follow-up conversation.

## Schedule a Dedicated Career Fulfillment Conversation

**Purpose:** Career development deserves its own space. After your Reflections Conversation, plan a dedicated meeting to talk about your career goals and aspirations.

**Schedule and Prepare:**

- **Set up a meeting:** Within the next 3 months, schedule time with your manager to focus on your career development.
- **Discuss your goals:** Share your career aspirations, strengths, and areas for growth.
- **Explore opportunities:** Talk about stretch assignments, visibility, and ways to increase your impact.
- **Ask for support:** Discuss how your manager can help you build new skills, connect with mentors, or advocate for your goals.
- **Use available resources:** Check out the Career Fulfillment resources on the Hub.

## FAQ

**1. How often do I complete Reflections?**

At least twice per fiscal year (once in H1: July–December, once in H2: January–May), or more frequently as requested by your manager.

**2. How are Reflections used to determine impact?**

Managers use Reflections, 1:1s, feedback, and observations to understand your full impact for the fiscal year.

**3. How is inclusion considered in Reflections?**

We have shifted from a standalone DI&B priority to embedding inclusion in how you work, interact, and lead. Reflect on behaviors that exemplify GitHub's culture and values and document them in your Reflection.

**4. How should I address security in my Reflection, regardless of my role?**

All Hubbers should describe how they've prioritized security—whether by building secure products, protecting data, or driving security awareness. Reflect on your commitment to security, actions taken, and plans to further strengthen security in your work.

Examples include reporting risks, leading security initiatives, ensuring secure design, raising customer concerns, completing security training, and integrating security into daily work. Tailor your examples to your role and collaborate with your manager if needed.

**5. What if my deliverables take many months and don't show immediate results?**

Focus on milestones achieved and their impact. Discuss ongoing work, collaboration, and growth mindset.

**6. I had circumstances outside my control — projects postponed or canceled, customers unavailable — how do I capture this in my self-reflection?**

As you write your self-reflection, capture the changes and speak to the adjustments you made and the impact of any new goals added during the time period of this Reflection.

**7. Who has visibility to my written self-reflection?**

Your self-reflection in Workday is initially visible only to you. Once you submit it to your manager they will be able to see your input and respond to your responses in Workday. Once your manager submits it in the tool, you will be able to see your manager's comments. These exchanges are also visible by your entire management chain.

**8. What if … I'm new? Transitioned to a new role? Returned from leave?**

| Situation | Guidance |
|-----------|----------|
| **New Hire** | **Timing:** Within 30 days of start date. **Focus:** Set expectations and goals for the next 3-6 months. **Reflection scope:** Complete question 3 — set goals. |
| **Hubber who transitioned to my team** | **Timing:** Coordinate with prior manager. **Focus:** Reflect on past contributions and align on future goals. **Reflection scope:** Prior manager completes questions 1 and 2 (if unavailable, see if manager of manager has comments). Hubber completes questions 1-3. Current manager completes question 3 — set goals. |
| **Return from extended LOA (> 30 days)** | **Timing:** Within 30 days of return. **Focus:** Reflect on contributions prior to leave and establish goals for the next 3-6 months. **Reflection scope:** Manager and Hubber document accomplishments prior to leave (question 1). Manager and Hubber complete question 3 — set goals. |

**9. What should I consider if I want to use text generative AI tools to help me with Reflections?**

Microsoft Copilot for Microsoft 365 can help you organize your content, generate ideas, and summarize accomplishments across Outlook, OneNote, and Teams. However, you are responsible for ensuring all AI-assisted content is accurate and complete.

- Always review, edit, and validate anything AI drafts—human judgment is essential.
- Managers and Hubbers must ensure their Reflections are accurate; do not use Copilot to assess, rank, or infer performance.
- GitHub does not provide dedicated HR AI tools for Reflections. Use AI-generated content thoughtfully and never copy/paste without review.
- Follow Microsoft's Responsible AI guidance and data use policies.
- Adhere to Microsoft's Responsible Use of Technology and Microsoft's Access to Business and Non-Business Related Data policy.
