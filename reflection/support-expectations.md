# Support Engineer Role Expectations & Promotion Guidance

This document provides guidance on Support Engineer levels, expectations, and the promotion process to help inform your reflections and career planning.

---

## Support Engineer Levels Overview

GitHub Support has multiple Individual Contributor (IC) levels for Support Engineers with specific metric expectations:

---

### Support Engineer II (IC3 / Level 7)
**Focus:** Building foundational skills and contributing to team success

#### Metric Expectations

| Metric | Weight | Evaluation Criteria |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Solved tickets | 30% | Compared to squad baseline |
| Ticket Resolution Time | 20% | Efficiency compared to squad baseline |
| Ticket complexity | 10% | Compared to squad baseline |
| Documentation contribution | 10% | KB and public docs contributions |
| Collaboration & Team Support | 10% | Slack help, projects, interviews, buddy work |
| Training & Skill Development | 20% | Required training, certifications, badging |

#### Core Expectations
- Handle customer tickets independently with minimal guidance
- Help team maintain minimum 4.5 CSAT and 95% IR Met
- Provide screen shares/calls when appropriate
- Complete required training and GitHub certifications
- Contribute to documentation and knowledge sharing
- Demonstrate growth mindset in learning new technologies

**Impact Scope:** Individual contributor delivering consistent results

---

### Support Engineer III (IC4 / Level 8)
**Focus:** Increasing technical depth and squad contribution

#### Metric Expectations

| Metric | Weight | Evaluation Criteria |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Solved tickets | 20% | Compared to squad baseline |
| Ticket Resolution Time | 20% | Efficiency compared to squad baseline |
| Ticket complexity | 20% | Compared to squad baseline |
| Documentation contribution | 15% | KB and public docs contributions |
| Collaboration & Team Support | 15% | Slack help, projects, change proposals |
| Training & Skill Development | 10% | Required training, certifications, badging |

#### Core Expectations
- Help team maintain minimum 4.5 CSAT and 95% IR Met
- Prioritize squad-specific crisis responses
- Share ideas via Issues, PRs, and Support Change Proposals
- Keep support documentation up to date
- Gain expertise across product suites relevant to squad

**Impact Scope:** Strong individual contributor with increasing squad involvement

---

### Senior Support Engineer (IC4 / Level 9)
**Focus:** Demonstrating expertise and amplifying team impact

#### Metric Expectations

| Metric | Weight | Evaluation Criteria |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Solved tickets | 15% | Compared to squad baseline |
| Ticket Resolution Time | 15% | Efficiency compared to squad baseline |
| Ticket complexity | 20% | Compared to squad baseline |
| Documentation contribution | 20% | KB and public docs contributions |
| Collaboration & Team Support | 20% | Process improvements, mentoring, projects |
| Training & Skill Development | 10% | Training and enablement contributions |

#### Core Expectations
- Handle complex technical issues across multiple product areas
- Help with ticket triage and pair with engineers on complex tickets
- Help team understand and adopt changes effectively
- Improve squad velocity through playbooks, KB content, ticket smashes
- Act as squad liaison with EPD for product issues/supportability
- Direct/own project work within team (Chatops, Zendesk Views, etc.)
- Contribute to tooling/product (PRs, insightful Issues)
- Author and maintain support documentation

**Impact Scope:** Individual excellence + team enablement

---

### Staff Support Engineer (IC5 / Level 10)
**Focus:** Strategic impact and organization-wide influence

#### Metric Expectations

| Metric | Weight | Evaluation Criteria |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Solved tickets | 15% | Compared to squad baseline |
| Ticket Resolution Time | 15% | Efficiency compared to squad baseline |
| Ticket complexity | 20% | Compared to squad baseline |
| Documentation contribution | 20% | KB and public docs contributions |
| Collaboration & Team Support | 25% | Leadership, cross-team initiatives |
| Training & Skill Development | 5% | Training delivery and enablement |

#### Core Expectations

**Ticket Work:**
- Carry consistent complex ticket workload requiring nuanced judgment
- Act as primary contact for technical escalations within squad
- Engage in triage, trend analysis, and process improvement
- Own Squad SME role on EPD triage, collaborate with SEEs
- Monitor Engineering escalation dashboard for long-running issues

**Documentation & Communication:**
- Coach squad members on documentation processes
- Teach and standardize internal and external doc creation
- Maintain up-to-date squad documentation in repos and channels
- Communicate key squad updates across teams

**Process Improvement & Collaboration:**
- Identify and drive process improvements across squads
- Encourage open communication to remove barriers
- Lead cross-team initiatives, document outcomes
- Facilitate cross-functional partnerships

**Project Work & Initiatives:**
- Lead squad-specific work to reduce TTR and IPX
- Act as expert mentor on squad and cross-team projects
- Drive squad involvement in release readiness and supportability
- Represent squad needs and technical direction

**Squad Health & Meetings:**
- Monitor squad health and momentum, facilitate engagement
- Represent squad in ticket smashes, release readiness, triage meetings
- Surface trends, gaps, and improvement opportunities

**Outcomes:**
- Timely resolution of squad escalations
- Active mentorship and training delivery
- Continuous process improvement documentation
- Transparent reporting of squad health and trends

**Impact Scope:** Organization-wide impact and strategic contributions

## Promotion Process

### Key Principles
- **Impact-Based:** Promotions are based on demonstrable impact at the next level, not time in role
- **Evidence Required:** You must show consistent performance at the next level
- **Manager Support:** Your manager will advocate for you based on evidence of impact
- **Timing:** Promotions typically happen during annual review cycles, but can occur at other times

### Steps to Promotion

1. **Understand Next-Level Expectations**
   - Review the expectations for the level you're targeting
   - Discuss with your manager what success looks like
   - Identify gaps between current performance and next level

2. **Build Your Case**
   - Document examples of impact at the next level
   - Gather feedback and recognition from peers, other teams
   - Track metrics that demonstrate increased scope and impact
   - Contribute to projects beyond your core responsibilities

3. **Regular Check-ins**
   - Discuss promotion readiness in 1:1s with your manager
   - Get feedback on specific areas for growth
   - Adjust your focus based on manager guidance

4. **Formal Review**
   - Manager submits promotion packet with evidence
   - Leadership reviews for consistency across org
   - Decision communicated and, if approved, effective next cycle

### What Makes a Strong Promotion Case

**For IC3 → IC4:**
- Consistently handling complex issues independently
- Mentoring newer team members
- Contributing to team processes and tools
- Demonstrating expertise in specific product areas
- Exceeding individual metrics consistently

**For IC4 → IC5:**
- Driving initiatives that impact multiple teams
- Strategic thinking about support challenges
- Building partnerships with engineering/product
- Creating resources that scale knowledge org-wide
- Demonstrated thought leadership and influence

| Solved tickets | **30%** | 20% | 15% | 15% |

## Support Delivery Expectations

### Core Performance Areas
- **CSAT:** Help team maintain minimum 4.5 customer satisfaction
- **IR Met:** Help team maintain minimum 95% initial response met
- **Response Quality:** Screen shares/calls when appropriate to progress troubleshooting
- **Squad Health:** Contribute to overall squad velocity and effectiveness

### Beyond the Numbers
While metrics matter, promotions also consider:
- **Technical Depth:** How complex are the issues you solve?
- **Scope of Impact:** Are you helping one customer or enabling many?
- **Initiative:** Do you wait for assignments or identify opportunities?
- **Leadership:** Do you help others succeed?
- **Squad Contribution:** How do you improve squad velocity and knowledge?

### Reporting & Self-Assessment
- Use the **SE Comparison Report in PowerBI** to track progress against squad baselines
- Compare your metrics to squad averages worldwide
- Align impact with manager guidance regularly

## Using This in Your Reflection

When writing your reflection, consider:

1. **What level are you currently at?** Frame your accomplishments in that context
2. **What level are you aiming for?** Show evidence of performing at that level
3. **How does your work demonstrate the expectations?** Use specific examples
4. **What metrics support your impact?** Quantify where possible
5. **How have you grown?** Show progression over time

### Example Framing

**IC3 Example:**
"I independently resolved 120+ tickets this half, including complex billing and enterprise deployment issues. I also mentored two new team members during onboarding, creating documentation that reduced their ramp-up time by 30%."

**IC4 Example:**
"I led the Access Squad's response to the Universe rollout, coordinating across three teams to ensure smooth customer migration. This included creating triage documentation that reduced escalations by 25% and training 15 support engineers on new features."

**IC5 Example:**
"I identified a pattern in escalations related to SSO configuration and partnered with the Enterprise team to redesign our documentation and tooling. This cross-functional initiative reduced SSO-related tickets by 40% org-wide and improved CSAT scores for enterprise customers."

## AI Usage in Support

GitHub Support's long-term AI goal is to creatively transform how we work, boosting efficiency by offloading repetitive tasks to AI. This enables humans to focus on impactful, nuanced areas. Currently prioritizing building strong AI usage habits.

### AI Usage Expectations by Performance Level

#### Below Expectations
**Behaviors:**
- Avoids using Copilot or AI for tickets even when it could improve efficiency
- Little to no learning or engagement with AI tools
- Apathetic or vocally unsupportive of AI adoption

**Indicators:**
- Below average use of Zendesk CGS (Copilot Generated Solutions)
- Little to no use of Doc-It and Copilot Agent mode for documentation
- Little to no participation in Lunch and Learn and AI training initiatives

**Action Required:** Needs coaching and training/upskilling to align with baseline expectations

---

#### Baseline (Expected Standard)
**Behaviors:**
- Actively uses GitHub Copilot in daily workflow, reviews and personalizes responses
- Uses AI for suggestions, validates output, complies with guidelines
- Provides feedback to improve Copilot/AI quality and efficiency
- Actively working toward or has obtained GitHub Copilot certification

**Indicators:**
- Average use of Zendesk CGS
- Regular feedback provided to improve Copilot/AI
- Active participation in existing resources and tools (Doc-It, Copilot Agent mode)
- Active participation in Lunch and Learn and AI training initiatives

**Standard:** This represents the expected baseline for all team members

---

#### Exceeding Expectations
**Behaviors:**
- Proactively learns and applies advanced AI features
- Accelerates resolution and provides feedback for AI improvements
- Champions, coaches, and mentors peers on creative AI use
- Identifies trends and uses AI for proposed solutions
- Customizes AI tools to better fit team needs

**Indicators:**
- Exceeding usage of Zendesk CGS
- Acts as an AI Champion to improve AI integration and facilitate discussions
- Coaches/mentors Lunch and Learn sessions and AI training initiatives
- Number of innovative solutions implemented using AI

**Impact:** Sets standard for the team, driving innovation and efficiency

---

### AI Tools in Support
- **GitHub Copilot** (in repos & Zendesk)
- **Doc-It** (documentation assistance)
- **Copilot Agent mode** (documentation contributions)
- **Zendesk CGS** (Copilot Generated Solutions)
- **ChatGPT and advanced analytics tools**
- **Copilot Spaces** (custom knowledge bases)

### Tracking AI Contributions
- Engagement with CGS/Copilot for Zendesk tracked via Azure Data Explorer dashboard
- Managers have access to usage metrics
- Broader access being developed for all support staff
- Official usage metrics will be available to individuals
- Similar dashboards may be developed for other tools (Doc-It, Spaces, etc.)

### Documenting AI Usage in Reflections

When writing about AI usage, consider these areas:

**Personal Productivity:**
- How did you use AI to work more efficiently?
- What time savings or quality improvements resulted?

**Customer Impact:**
- Did AI help you solve problems faster or better?
- How did it improve customer experience?

**Team Enablement:**
- Did you share AI techniques or tools with others?
- Did you create resources others could use?

**Innovation:**
- Did you identify new use cases or create AI-powered solutions?
- Did you contribute to improving AI tools?

**Examples:**
- "Used GitHub Copilot to quickly generate test scripts, reducing issue reproduction time by 50%"
- "Created Copilot Space for common Access Squad scenarios, adopted by 8 team members"
- "Leveraged AI tools to analyze log patterns, identifying root cause in complex debugging scenarios"
- "Provided 25+ feedback items on Zendesk CGS accuracy, resulting in improved suggestions for billing issues"
- "Mentored 3 team members on advanced Copilot features during lunch and learn session"
- "Used Doc-It to contribute 15+ documentation updates, reducing documentation time by 40%"
