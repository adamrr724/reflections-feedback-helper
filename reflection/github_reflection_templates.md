# GitHub Reflection Templates and Guidance

This document combines GitHub's internal reflection templates and best practices to help you create compelling self-reflections.

## GitHub Project Impact Template

Use this structured approach for highlighting business impact in your reflections:

### What:
[Brief summary of what you created, implemented, or led. Include teams you collaborated with and the specific deliverable(s).]

**Example:** Created and implemented the [Project/Framework Name] with [Teams/Stakeholders], including [Tools or Platforms Used].

### Why:
[Explain the gap, problem, or strategic need. What was missing or ineffective before this work?]

**Example:** Prior to this work, there were no [relevant measures, systems, or clarity] to support [business goal/team need], limiting [stakeholder group's] ability to [action/outcome].

### Impact:
[Describe the measurable or strategic outcome. How did this change things for the business, customers, or internal teams?]

**Example:** This framework now enables [Stakeholder/Team] to [action/insight], aligning with [broader organizational goals] and mirroring established success metrics from [related teams or departments].

### 🛠 How I Did It: (Optional)
[Summarize techniques, processes, or innovations used to drive the work forward.]

* Used [methodology or tool] to [action/outcome]
* Facilitated [type of sessions] to gather feedback
* Leveraged AI tools (e.g., Copilot) to [action]

### 🌟 Additional Contributions & Recognition: (Optional)
* Created reusable artifacts (e.g., GitHub Issues, templates) that are now used as models by other teams
* Recognized by [person/team] for [specific aspect]
* Work is showcased as a best practice for [group or initiative]

## Reflection Best Practices from GitHub Teams

### Key Principles
- **Quality over quantity:** Keep reflections focused, concise, and easier to write
- **Your impact on team, org, and company priorities:** Show what impact you had, and how you made it
- **Goals:** Set goals for the impact you'd like to have on current priorities and how you want to grow next

### High-Quality Feedback Framework
When giving or requesting feedback, focus on:
- **What:** Highlight progress and results toward team and/or business goals
- **How:** The behaviors and actions that enhanced their impact, brought culture to life, and contributed to a high-performing team
- **Keep it concise:** Use 1-2 specific sentences
- **Use SBI method:** Situation-Behavior-Impact for structured feedback

## Two-Part Reflection Structure

### Part 1: Looking Back
**Question to answer:** "What did I do that had a net positive impact for GitHub?"

**Guidelines:**
- Link to work you've done, but balance granularity
- Don't link every PR - focus on epics/batches with impact descriptions
- Provide clear understanding of:
  - Work you did
  - What it intended to achieve  
  - Results you delivered
  - Net positive impact

**Example Structure:**
> My work on [project] contributed to [specific outcome] as visible on [dashboard/metric]. The work was tracked in [epic link] and communicated in [team posts/announcements]. This work contributed to [improved workflows/availability/business impact].

### Part 2: Going Forward
**Question to answer:** "What are my key plans for the next cycle and how will I measure success?"

**Guidelines:**
- Provide insights into key items for next cycle
- Include aspirational goals beyond immediate quarter
- Detail how you'll measure success
- Link to initiatives/ADRs for concrete insights

## Reflection Writing Tips

### Process Recommendations
1. **Set aside dedicated time** - Block calendar to avoid interruptions
2. **Write in Google Docs first** - Then copy to final destination for better editing experience
3. **Use templates** - Leverage structured templates for consistency
4. **Review with manager** - Get alignment on priorities and impact

### Content Guidelines
- Focus on **business impact** over activities
- Use **specific metrics and outcomes** where possible
- Highlight **collaboration and cross-team work**
- Include **learning and growth** opportunities
- Show **progression toward company priorities**

### Links to Include
- Epic/project tracking links
- Dashboards showing impact
- Team announcements/communications
- ADRs (Architecture Decision Records)
- Reusable artifacts you created
